Sketch Narrative

Context: This sketch visualizes a taxi passing by as seen by a runner during a city run. It lacks a lot of visual cues besides the striking yellow of the taxi as a runners main focus wouldn't be to focus on minor details.

  • Context: Runners would likely see a taxi on the street when running through a big city.
  • Design decisions:
    • I designed the scene to be pretty abstract due to my limited knowledge of p5.js and to match with the rest of the clocks.
    • I decided to keep it fairly simple more of a "at a glance" view of a taxi passing by, this is due to the fact that most runners would likely not focus alot on a taxi passing by to get more details of it. Think more of a pov from a runner.
    • I also decided to have the speed of the taxi be moving forward once every second to add another aspect to the sketch.
  • Future work:
    • Really adding more visual elements to make the scene more engaging. If I learn more of p5.js I would love to make it better visually.

Sketch Narrative

Context: This sketch visualizes a neighborhood barbershop as seen by a runner passing by during a city run. It focuses on how familiar storefront elements, are briefly perceived in motion, capturing how runners pick up quick visual cues while moving through the urban environment.

  • Context: Runners would likely pass by many businesses while running for example a barbershop.
  • Design decisions:
    • I designed the scene to be pretty abstract due to my limited knowledge of p5.js and to match with the rest of the clocks.
    • I decided to not listen to advice on add more information and utilizing the space because I thought having this more simple reflects what runners would see briefly during their run.
  • Future work:
    • Potentially exploring having more information such as date.
    • Adding more visuals such as the spinning white and red on barbershops.

Sketch Narrative

Context: This sketch visualizes what a runner sees on their smartwatch during a run, showing key metrics like time, pace, distance, and heart rate in a quick, glanceable layout. It addresses the need for runners to monitor performance at a glance without breaking focus or stopping mid-run.

  • Context: Runners would likely be always using a watch regardless of where.
  • Design decisions:
    • Kept the relative background really simple in order to show focus on the watch face as well as my limited experience with p5.js.
    • I chose pace, distance, and heart rate as the metrics displayed because they are the main three runners would want to see.
  • Future work:
    • Adding option to add more metrics to the watch face.
